Indian Mutton Curry

This simple yet delicious version of mutton is easy to put together.
A mix of sauteed onion, tomatoes, garlic and onions is pureed to make a flavourful sauce with a perfect balance of savory, aromatic spices. Dried spices add an unbeatable depth of flavor as the ingredients simmer together. Goat curry is delicious served with steamed rice, a salad and raita. If you thicken the gravy, it tastes just as good with hot, freshly made chapatis

Ingredients:

  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 large onions,sliced thin
  • 2 large tomatoes,diced
  • 2 tablespoons garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala powder
  • 2 tablespoons coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 2 pounds goat(or sheep leg),cut into bite-sized chunks
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Chopped fresh coriander,garnish

Steps:

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a heavy-bottom large pan over medium heat.
  2. When hot, add the onions. Saute until the onions begin to turn a plae golden brown. Remove from the oil with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Turn off the heat but leave the remaining oil in the pan
  3. Grind the onions into a smooth paste(adding very little to no water) in a food processor. Once smooth, move into a separate container.
  4. Grind the tomatoes and garlic and ginger pastes together in the food processor into a smooth paste. Set aside for later use.
  5. Heat the oil over medium heat leftover from frying the onions and add the onion paste. Saute for 2 to 3 minutes
  6. Add the tomato, garlic and ginger paste followed by the garam masala, coriander, cumin, turmeric and red chilli powder. Mix well.
  7. Saute the resulting masala until the oil begin to separate from it. This can take up to 10 minutes
  8. Add the goat or sheep pieces to the masala, season with salt to taste, and stir to fully coat the meat with the masala. Saute until the goat or sheep is browned well.
  9. Add 1/2 a cup of hot water to the pan and stir to mix well. Bring to a simmer and cover the pan. Cook until the meat is tender. You will need to keep checking on the goat or sheep as it cooks and adding more water to the sauce if all the water dries up. Stir often to prevent burning. The dish should have a fairly thick grav when done.
  10. When the mean is cooked and the sauce has thickened, garnish with chopped coriander and serve hot
